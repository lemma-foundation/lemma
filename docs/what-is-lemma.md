# What Is Lemma?

Lemma is a Verified Reasoning Network.

It turns hard reasoning tasks into verified training data. Miners search for solutions. Validators check those solutions with deterministic verifiers. Accepted solutions become public data that future AI systems can learn from.

## The Short Version

AI can guess. Verifiers can check. Lemma pays for checked reasoning.

## Why This Matters

Modern AI can produce answers that sound correct. Lemma focuses on answers that can be mechanically checked.

A proof that passes a verifier is more useful than a claim that sounds plausible. It can be replayed, audited, retrieved, deduplicated, attributed, and used as training data.

## Why Lean First

Lean theorem proving is the only production domain today. Lean gives Lemma a mature deterministic checker: a proof either passes in the pinned environment or it fails.

Math is the wedge. Verified reasoning data is the product.

## What Lemma Produces

Lemma produces the Lemma Corpus: replayable rows of verified reasoning data. A row records the task, proof, verifier metadata, source and license metadata, attribution, dependencies, and verification result.

The current corpus is Lean proof data. Failed proofs are not corpus rows. Valid alternate proofs can be stored when they add useful proof diversity, even when they do not earn duplicate reward.

## What Lemma Is Not

Lemma is not a prose-judging network, a generic code benchmark, a smart-contract escrow product, or a Google DeepMind Formal Conjectures payout path.

Lemma is independent and is not endorsed by Google DeepMind. Formal Conjectures and related benchmarks are research context and evaluation targets, not the v1 reward path.

Non-Lean domains are not active yet. Verus, SAT/SMT, LP/SDP, and cryptanalysis are roadmap examples only.
